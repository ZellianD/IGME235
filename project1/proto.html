The Endocrine System

The endocrine system consists of the body's glands and the hormones they secrete. It regulates the biological process of the body from conception through old age. It monitors the development of all other systems of the body.

The Pituitary Gland

Receives signals from the hypothalamus (which connects the nervous and endocrine systems together and drives the endocrine system) It’s hormones control other glands such as he thyroid gland, adrenal glands, and the reproductive glands Controls how the body grows The posterior lobe produces vasopressin and oxytocin

The Pineal Gland

Manages the sleep cycle and releases melatonin

The Thyroid Gland

Regulates the body’s metabolism Growth and development of the body

The Parathyroid Gland

Control the level of calcium, phosphorus, and magnesium in the body Calcium is needed for the heart, kidneys, bones, and nervous system

The Adrenal Gland

Sits right on top of the kidneys Control metaplasm, immune system, blood pressure, sexual development Response to stress

The Pancreas

Plays a significant role in the endocrine and digestive system Makes insulin Insulin controls the level of blood sugar in the blood

The Testes

Produce sperm Release the hormone testosterone which effects sperm production, muscle strength, and sex drive

The Ovaries

Releases eggs Release the sex hormone estrogen, progesterone, oestrogen, and testosterone Testosterone is supposed to be highest during ovulation

The endocrine system connects so heavily with other systems in the body that when learning about other systems the endocrine system must be taken into account. The endocrine system "works" most closly with the nervous system
